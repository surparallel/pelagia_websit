Let's Add One To The Four Necessary Conditions For Deadlock

We all know that the deadlock problem must be solved thoroughly under multithreading. Because the deadlock can't be found by conventional testing, and the first level bug causing software denial of service appears randomly in the project running.

The deadlock problem was proposed by e. g. Coffman, M. J. elpeck, a. shoshani in the paper "system deadlocks" in 1971. Four well-known solutions are given, which are called "four necessary conditions for deadlock".

“This deadlock situation has arisen only because all of the following general conditions were operative:

1. Tasks claim exclusive control of the resources they require ("mutual exclusion" condition).

2. Tasks hold resources already allocated to them while waiting for additional resources ("wait for" condition).

3. Resources cannot be forcibly removed from the tasks holding them until the resources are used to completion ("no preemption" condition).

4. A circular chain of tasks exists, such that each task holds one or more resources that are being requested by the next task in the chain ("circular wait" condition).”

These four conditions all have an implicit premise that task must run in different threads or processes. If this implied premise is not true, deadlock will not happen. Therefore, one of the four conditions lacks an implied necessary prerequisite. Tasks must be parallel before deadlock occurs.

Suppose that two tasks running in different threads or processes will deadlock due to two resources. If only two tasks are executed in one thread or process in turn, the deadlock problem will be solved.

That is, if you have 100 tasks, two of them deadlock. Put these two tasks in a single thread, and put the remaining 98 tasks in 98 threads respectively. Then we solve the deadlock problem without affecting the parallelism of the remaining 98 tasks.

This gives me a new inspiration, if we can find that two tasks have potential deadlock. Putting them back into a thread can completely solve the problem of software deadlock.
